Location & Travel Details

Location: Shree Siddhivinayak Ganapati Temple, SK Bole Marg, Prabhadevi, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400028 Nearest Railway Station: Dadar (around 2 km away) Nearest Metro Station: Dadar Metro station(around 2 km away) Distance from Mumbai Airport: ~11 km Landmark: It is near Dadar Worli junction

Travel Info:By Local Train Nearest “big” station: Dadar (on both Western & Central lines).
Also Prabhadevi Railway Station (former Elphinstone Road) on Western line is quite close. From Dadar, about 1.5-2 km. Auto/taxi ~5-10 mins depending on traffic. Walking ~15-20 mins. By Metro There is a Siddhivinayak Metro Station on the Aqua Line (Metro 3) that is super close (~0.1 km) to the temple. Very handy if you’re using metro. Saves you from traffic jams. By Bus BEST buses run from different parts of Mumbai (Western, Central, Harbour lines) to Prabhadevi / Dadar / stops very near the temple (e.g. “Siddhivinayak Mandir” bus stop or “Ravindra Natya Mandir”) Buses are cheap. Time depends heavily on traffic. Might take longer during peak hours. By Road / Taxi / Auto-rickshaw From the airport (Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Int’l Airport) ~10-12 km. Taxi / ride-share (Uber / Ola etc.) possible.
If coming from different parts of the city, using cabs may be easier since it takes you close. Travel time from airport ~30-45 min depending on traffic. Cost will vary. Autos/taxis from nearby train/metro station might cost ₹50-₹150 depending on distance.

Visitor Tips and Timings

Timings: Opening hours: The temple opens early (around 5:30 AM most days) and stays open till about 9:50 PM. On Tuesdays the timings are special (opens even earlier) due to extra devotional activity. Least crowded times: Early morning right at opening is your best bet. Avoid Tuesdays, weekends, and festival days if you want less waiting.

Tips:Parking: Very limited and difficult near the temple. If possible avoid driving yourself; use public transport or drop-off via taxi. Security: There are security checks. Also note that recent bans / restrictions (e.g. on large offerings like coconuts or certain flowers) are sometimes in place. Best to check current rules before you go. What to carry: Light bag, water, maybe something to cover shoulders/knees if you want to enter the inner areas (just in case). Also, carry small change for autos/buses. Appointments / Tickets: Historically, darshan is free and walk-in; paid “fast darshan” is optional. Sometimes there are slot bookings, but often walk-in works. It never hurts to check their official site/or app ahead.
